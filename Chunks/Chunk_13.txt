13. Chunk
Chunk title: FTC says Buyers Guide overrides conflicting contract language

Source title: FTC — Buying a Used Car From a Dealer

Source URL: https://consumer.ftc.gov/articles/buying-used-car-dealer

 

Summary

The FTC explains that if the Buyers Guide says the car comes with a warranty, but the sales contract says the car is sold “as is,” the dealer must give the warranty described in the Buyers Guide. Cars.ai should use this chunk when helping a shopper interpret confusing paperwork or protect themselves during negotiation. This is valuable for long-term ownership questions because warranty protection can significantly affect post-purchase cost and risk. The assistant should encourage users to make sure any negotiated warranty terms appear on the Buyers Guide and not rely on spoken promises alone. This chunk is important because it turns document review into a real buying decision factor.
