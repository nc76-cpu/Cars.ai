1. Chunk
Chunk title: NHTSA 5-Star Safety Ratings overview

Source title: NHTSA — Car Safety Ratings | Vehicles, Car Seats, Tires

Source URL: https://www.nhtsa.gov/ratings

 

Summary

NHTSA’s 5-Star Safety Ratings are meant to help consumers make safety-focused decisions when buying a vehicle. The ratings system lets users search vehicle safety ratings and compare vehicles by manufacturer. NHTSA also provides a vehicle comparison tool that shows 5-Star Safety Ratings together with recall information, which makes it useful for side-by-side shopping. For Cars.ai, this chunk should be used whenever a user asks whether a car is “good” from a safety perspective. The assistant should explain that this source is appropriate for crash-safety comparison, not for resale value or mechanical reliability. If two vehicles are otherwise similar, stronger NHTSA ratings can be treated as a point in favor of the safer choice. The assistant should not overstate the rating as the only factor; it should combine NHTSA ratings with recalls, IIHS results, fuel economy, and buying-risk guidance from other chunks.
